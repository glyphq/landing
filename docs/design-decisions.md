# Design decisions

## Direction

The physical scene is a carefully typeset systems manual being used beside a working desktop application in neutral office light. The site defaults to the visitor’s system theme and remains high-contrast in both modes.

## Identity

- Typography and ruled structure carry most of the identity.
- The header uses a temporary typographic `glyph.` treatment because a canonical vector wordmark was not present. Approved repository raster assets are retained under `public/brand/` but are not used as reconstructed vector masters.
- Product colors appear as narrow identifiers, not page backgrounds.
- The oxblood Wallet accent anchors the system. Other accents remain subdued and accessible.

## Composition

The homepage hero is deliberately text-only: one primary statement, supporting copy, and actions. System structure appears later through ruled rows and diagrams, where it carries explanatory value. Pages use long horizontal rules, asymmetric editorial columns, structured rows, and diagrams rather than repeated card grids.

## Impeccable amplification

The final pass increases impact through the existing system rather than adding effects. The mission becomes a full-width oxblood statement, current products receive decisive accent rules, diagrams use stronger product-coded hierarchy, evidence sections alternate density and open space, and the closing action becomes a dark terminal point. Heroes remain text-only and buttons do not move on hover.

## Current-product evidence

Wallet uses an abstracted UI composition, explicitly not a fake screenshot, because no repository screenshot or safely runnable test vault was available during implementation. Connect code follows its published v2.0.0 API. Future products use architecture, stated principles, and unavailable-now language instead of invented interfaces.
